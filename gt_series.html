OVERVIEW:

The GT Series is the first ever Gran Touring production car created by Epsilon. This piece of history is no longer used in GT racing, but represents Epsilon in other racing events. The GT Series has innovated the racing industry with its Formula 1 inspired downforce system. The sleek design allows for minimal drag and the ability to take corners with speed. All GT Series cars produced have been modified to support comfort. Replacing the hard, light racing seats with comfortable bucket seats suitable for casual use. The reworked visually appealing dashboard replaces the bland, lightweight racing one.

advanced downforce 
glued to the ground

The GT Series' underside of the car includes diffusers directing airflow to the sides. Instead of a straight rear diffuser guiding air from under the car, Epsilon's system sends air out the flanks. By pushing the air out, a low pressure zone is created underneath the car. The low pressure zone sucks the car to the bottom of the road. This creates more downforce without the added weight of a large wing or splitters.

downforce equals speed

The success of the GT Series is a result of the downforce system. Forcing the car to the ground allows for more traction between tyres and the track. The increased traction allows the vehicle to have a higher top speed and take turns at a faster velocity.
